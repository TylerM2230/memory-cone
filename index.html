Bergson's Memory Cone

The Concept

Henri Bergson used the metaphor of a cone to describe the nature of memory and its relationship to perception and action. It illustrates how our entire past coexists, virtually, and informs our present experience. Use your mouse (click and drag) to rotate the cone and scroll to zoom.

Refers to: Overall Visualization

The Cone Volume

The volume of the cone represents the entirety of our accumulated memories, our "pure memory". It contains all past experiences, existing virtually. The memories are layered, with older, more general memories towards the apex and newer, more detailed memories towards the base.

Refers to: The Blue Layered Cone

The Apex (Point 'S')

The apex or point (labeled 'S' in Bergson's diagrams) represents the most contracted state of memory – the deepest, most consolidated, and abstract past. It's the furthest point from immediate action.

Refers to: The Shiny Gold Sphere at the Point (Tip)

The Base (Plane 'P')

The circular base represents the present moment – the "plane of action and perception". This is where memory intersects with the current sensory-motor experience. Memories relevant to the present situation are "actualized" or brought forth onto this plane to guide action.

Refers to: The Gold Circular Plane at the Cone Base

Interaction

Our consciousness constantly moves between the apex (pure memory) and the base (present action). When we act, we are primarily at the base. When we dream or reminisce deeply, we move closer to the apex. The cone visualizes this dynamic interplay between past and present.
